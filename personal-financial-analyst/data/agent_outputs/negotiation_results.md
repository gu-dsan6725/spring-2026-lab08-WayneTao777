## 1. INTERNET/CABLE SERVICE

### Why It Works
Providers have high customer acquisition costs; they'd rather keep you at a discount than lose you to a competitor.

### Best Timing
- End of month/quarter
- When intro promotional rate expires (every 12 months)
- Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11 AM or 4–5 PM

### Exact Script
> "Hi, I've been a customer for [X years], but my bill has increased to $[amount] and [Competitor] offers similar speeds for $[X]. Before I switch, can you connect me with someone in retention?"

**Then in retention:**
> "What promotional rates or bundle options can you offer me to keep my business? I've seen [Provider A] at $49/month for 400 Mbps."

### Ask For
- $15–30/month rate reduction for 12 months
- Free premium channels (HBO Max, etc.) for 6 months
- Waived equipment rental fees ($8–15/month)
- One-time $100–200 account credit

### Expected Savings
- Realistic: $25–35/month → **$300–420/year**

## 2. CAR INSURANCE

### Why It Works
Insurance is highly competitive; loyalty discounts are often given ONLY when customers ask.

### Best Timing
- 30–60 days before policy renewal
- With competitor quotes in hand (Geico, State Farm, Progressive, USAA)

### Exact Script
> "I'm calling about my renewal on [date]. I've been a customer for [X years], but [Competitor] is quoting me $[X] for the same coverage I have now for $[Y]. Can you match or improve that quote?"

**Discount audit question:**
> "Can you review my policy and confirm I'm getting all applicable discounts—good driving record, bundling, safety features, low mileage, auto-pay, alumni associations, professional organizations?"

### Ask For
- Match competitor quote (5–15% reduction)
- Bundle discount (auto + home = 5–25% savings)
- Good driver, auto-pay, paperless discounts
- Higher deductible adjustment ($500→$1,000 = 5–15% savings)

### Expected Savings
- Realistic: $20–40/month → **$240–480/year**

## 3. CELL PHONE PLANS

### Why It Works
Customer acquisition costs far exceed retention costs; carriers will make deals rather than lose you.

### Best Timing
- End of contract/upgrade eligibility
- When competing carrier has a "switch incentive"

### Exact Script
> "I've been a customer since [date]. I've analyzed my usage—I use about [X GB] of data—and I'm paying for more than I need. Also, [T-Mobile/Competitor] has a promotion for $[X] for unlimited data. Can someone review my account for options?"

### Ask For
- Plan downgrade to right-sized tier
- $10–20/month loyalty discount
- Free international roaming passes
- Military, student, senior discounts
- Carrier switching payment if changing providers

### Expected Savings
- Realistic: $15–30/month → **$180–360/year**

## 4. CREDIT CARD APR REDUCTION

### Why It Works
Credit card companies profit on interest; losing a good customer to a balance transfer costs them more than a rate reduction.

### Best Timing
- After 6+ months of perfect on-time payments
- After receiving a competing 0% balance transfer offer

### Exact Script
> "I've been a cardholder for [X years] with an excellent payment history—never a missed payment. My APR is [X]%, and I've seen offers for [Y]% elsewhere. I'd prefer to stay, but could you lower my rate?"

**Present leverage:**
> "I carry a $[balance] balance—at current APR that's $[annual interest cost]/year. If you could lower my rate to [target], I'd keep this as my primary card."

### Ask For
- Permanent 3–5 point APR reduction
- Temporary 0% APR for 6–12 months
- Annual fee waiver
- One-time interest charge reversal

### Expected Savings (on $10K balance, 5-point reduction)
- Realistic: $75–150/month → **$900–1,800/year**

## 5. BANK FEES

### Why It Works
Banks prefer to retain customers over small fee disputes. Success rate: 85–90%.

### Exact Script (Overdraft)
> "I'm calling about an overdraft fee on [date] for $[amount]. I'm usually careful with my account, but had an unexpected expense. I've never requested a reversal before—would you be willing to reverse this charge?"

### Ask For
- Full fee reversal (almost always granted first time)
- Account type change to eliminate monthly fees
- Overdraft protection linked to savings account
- Balance alert setup (prevents future fees)

### Expected Savings
- $35 per reversed overdraft fee; elimination of monthly fees: **$60–420/year**

## 6. STREAMING SERVICES

### Why It Works
Maximum leverage is at the cancellation moment—services have built-in retention offers.

### Exact Script
> Simply begin the cancellation process online or by phone. Most services will offer a retention deal (free month, discount) BEFORE confirming cancellation. Evaluate and decide.

**If calling:**
> "I'm calling to cancel. I've enjoyed the service, but I'm not watching enough to justify the cost. Do you have any options to keep me as a subscriber at a lower price?"

### Expected Savings
- Realistic: $10–20/month across 1–2 services → **$120–240/year**

## Universal Negotiation Tips

### The 5 Power Phrases
1. *"I've been a loyal customer since [date] and I'd prefer to stay."*
2. *"[Competitor] is offering [specific price]. Before I switch, what can you do?"*
3. *"Can I speak with someone in your retention department?"*
4. *"Can you put that offer in writing so I have a record?"*
5. *"I'm ready to move forward if we can get to $[target]."*

### Always
- Have specific competitor quotes in writing before calling
- Call Tuesday–Thursday, mid-morning
- Get offers confirmed in writing before accepting
- Set calendar reminders to renegotiate every 12 months

### Never
- Threaten to cancel without being willing to follow through
- Accept verbal-only offers
- Negotiate the same bill more than 2x/year
